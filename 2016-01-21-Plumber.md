In a nutshell, simply use Plumber to fix the pipe when using Gulp. This lets you handle errors more elegantly and avoid interrupting any of your Gulp tasks running in the background.

So, say you have Gulp running and watching your files at the background. Then you edited your SASS file and saved it. Unbeknownst to you, you missed a semi-colon. Or you simply did something silly in the file that Gulp-Sass threw an error that stopped the server.
The usual course of action is - rerun Gulp serve
